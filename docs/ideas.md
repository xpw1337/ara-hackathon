# Ranked Hackathon Ideas

## 1. Parallel You

Best shot at `Overall Winner`.

### What it is

An AI personal computer that behaves like a living chief of staff. It remembers your commitments, notices what is slipping, and proactively gives you the next best action, a draft reply, and a warning before you miss something important.

### Why it stands out

- Most teams will build a chatbot.
- This feels like a computer with agency and continuity.
- Ara is built for persistent runtime and automations, so the platform fit is strong.

### What makes it novel

It is not just "AI with memory." It continuously converts scattered life context into execution pressure relief.

### Demo moment

Feed it messy real student life context. It returns:

- what to do now
- what can wait
- what is likely to go wrong
- the exact message you should send

## 2. Life in Parallel

Best shot at `Most Viral`.

### What it is

An AI computer that simulates the next 24 hours of your life across multiple paths:

- if I study tonight
- if I go out
- if I ship this project
- if I skip this meeting

### Why it stands out

- Extremely demoable
- Social-media-friendly concept
- Makes AI feel magical and personal

### Risk

Harder to make trustworthy in one day. It can drift into gimmick territory if the outputs feel random.

## 3. Ghostwriter for Real Life

Best shot at `Most Viral` with lower implementation risk.

### What it is

An AI computer that drafts messages in your voice based on real context:

- teammate follow-ups
- professor emails
- founder outreach
- apology texts

### Why it stands out

- Instant emotional payoff
- Great live demo
- Easy for judges to relate to

### Risk

Less differentiated unless you strongly emphasize persistent memory and timing.

## 4. Student Ops OS

Best shot at `Most Useful`.

### What it is

An always-on campus copilot that manages deadlines, class admin, project tasks, club logistics, and social coordination from one memory layer.

### Why it stands out

- Strong Johns Hopkins-specific story
- Real adoption angle
- Easy to explain

### Risk

Can sound incremental if the demo does not feel proactive.

## My recommendation

Build `Parallel You`.

It is the best blend of:

- emotional resonance
- technical credibility
- Ara-native product fit
- demo simplicity

If you want, we can sharpen it into one of these tighter versions:

- `Parallel You for Students`
- `Parallel You for Founders`
- `Parallel You for Researchers`
